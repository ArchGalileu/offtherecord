Off The Record • Record Club

What's a record club?

We're still working it out ourselves, but Off The Record is be a monthly event where a bunch of music-loving people get together to listen to a couple of records in full – no distractions, no interruptions – and then talk about them afterwards.

Where and when does it happen?

Off The Record is hosted by Coco Momo cafe in central London. It's an evening event, running from 7pm-9.30pm or so, with (so far) around 20 people attending.

Got a record to suggest?

Cool – we have some guidelines on hosting a playback / discussion below. Have a read and you'll eventually be able to submit your record suggestion where people can vote for it for an upcoming Off The Record session. Or if you really can't wait, email Matt with your nominations.

Who's behind this?

Off The Record is an experimental project by Matt Andrews, a music loving web developer. While it's far from the first event of its kind, we wanted to create something where people could make time for good music and hear people talk passionately about why it's great.

Session Guidelines

  • Sessions will cover two albums maximum. If you want to run a session (please do!), think about two records which complement one another: maybe two albums by the same artist at different points in her career, or maybe two standouts from a given genre or timeframe.
  • It might be good to come pre-prepared with some talking points about your nominations, for example: what do you think of the lyrics to "Ziggy Stardust"? How does the guitar interplay with the other instruments? Was the producer on vacation for the final two songs?! Write down a few notes to get things moving so we can chat after we're done listening.
  • If you were going to introduce an open-minded newbie to a favourite genre or artist, what albums would you pick? Think about how they'll come across to a new listener and contextualise them: before playing them, you could say a few words to talk about the social climate or musical landscape the album was recorded and released in. This is about appreciating music and learning why it's so great.
